# Mechanically Activated Elastomeric Composite Pavement for Passive Ice Mitigation

Author: Sai Kolichalam  
Rowan University – Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering

## Overview

This research proposes a novel passive pavement system capable of mitigating ice formation on winter roadways by converting traffic-induced mechanical deformation into thermal energy.

The system utilizes an elastomeric composite layer containing recycled crumb rubber, silicon carbide thermal fillers, carbon black, and SBS polymer modifiers to generate heat through viscoelastic dissipation.

## Key Contributions

- Passive road heating using viscoelastic energy dissipation
- Multilayer pavement architecture for efficient upward heat transfer
- Analytical modeling predicting ~25.4 kW/m³ heat generation
- Estimated 2–4°C road surface temperature increase
- Sustainable use of recycled tire materials

## Technologies / Concepts

- Viscoelastic hysteresis heating
- Crumb rubber modified asphalt
- Silicon carbide thermal fillers
- SBS polymer modification
- Thermal transport modeling
